                  Generic Pronouns

                  published by Konrad Sunday, the 3rd October of 2021 (CET)

                  When pronouns are unknown at the point in time the text is written, we run into a bunch of problems.
                  (how did we get into this situation? uhh, (ghost)writing y/n fiction (for a friend of course!!))

                  Not only do we have to consider the different pronoun forms: subjective, posessive and objective,
                  we also need to consider that they/them pronouns cause the auxillary verbs (be, have and do) to change into plural form üòñ

                  The best approach i see is to write our text with placeholder tags.
                  The computer recognizes $ followed by a unique string as a placeholder tag and will replace it at runtime, when we know which pronouns to use.
                  (c: ) tells the computer to capitalize the first letter.

                  Disclaimer: This is probably waaay to much effort for the benefit you gain, you could just avoid other charachters in your stories talking about yN or use they/them!!

                  Walking through an Example #

                  Ok, look at this:

(c: $they) $were even rubbing its belly and everything
I don't think it was even $their dog.
I also heard $they like$s to listen to audiobooks.

                  Pronoun variationts #

                  • $they - he/she/it/they comitted arson. [Subjective]
                  • $their - i borrowed his/her/their/its scarf [Posessive]
                  • $theirs - his/hers/theirs/‚Äúits‚Äù [Posessive2]
                  • $them - i gave him/her/it/them a knife. [Objective]
                  • [$them]self - ‚Ä¶ made it himself/herself/itself/themself (ves) [Objective]

                  Because of the godforsaken ‚Äòs‚Äô thats added in singular simple present, we need another exception

                  A very random DEMO, ‚Äì Story Source Snippet,

                  Basically, this macro does all the work.
                  It will define the variables once. They will then be available globally.

                  (set: $pronounGen to (macro: string-type _p, [
    (if: _p is "they/them")[
        (set: $are to "are")
        (set: $were to "were")
        (set: $have to "have")
        (set: $do to "do")
        (set: $s to "")
        (set: $re to "'re")
        (set: $ve to "'ve")
        
        (set: $they to "they")
        (set: $their to "their")
        (set: $theirs to "theirs")
        (set: $them to "them")
    ]
    (else:)[
        (set: $are to "is")
        (set: $were to "was")
        (set: $have to "has")
        (set: $do to "does")
        (set: $s to "s")
        (set: $re to "'s")
        (set: $ve to "'s")
    ]
    
    (if: _p is "he/him")[
        (set: $they to "he")
        (set: $their to "his")
        (set: $theirs to "his")
        (set: $them to "him")
    ]
    (else-if: _p is "she/her")[
        (set: $they to "she")
        (set: $their to "her")
        (set: $theirs to "hers")
        (set: $them to "her")
    ]  
    (output-data: (hidden:))
]))
